„Life while traveling is a dream in its purest form“

This summer, many of us had to look for an alternative to our usual vacation. Freelancers from Ufa Masha Gordeeva and Zhenya Mikhailov spent the whole summer traveling to picturesque places of Russia in their car. They shared their impressions, tips and life hacks with us.

From an early age, we traveled with our parents around Russia. This has always been a long-awaited event for us and always brought a lot of positive emotions. We mainly traveled around the Krasnodar territory and coastal towns. For the first few years, we usually got to the sea by train. There was always a very comfortable and pleasant atmosphere there. The closer we got to the southern climate, the more the landscape outside the window changed, and the more one could fall in love with the nature of the region. But then we started traveling by car, and it turned out that the same places can evoke different impressions.

When we were planning our trip, we immediately thought about what was important to us. We definitely need good internet to be able to work. We wanted to visit places with bodies of water to swim, and we also value privacy and silence. We planned to stay in motels and hostels for most of our trip, but we also took a tent with us so that we could sometimes spend the night outdoors. We decided to visit not the popular resort towns of Russia, but places with picturesque nature, rivers, and lakes. We chose Karelia and the Urals. We have friends in Yekaterinburg, and we were planning to organize an overnight river rafting trip.

We didn’t calculate the expenses in too much detail before the trip, but we didn’t want to spend a lot and we tried to save money, but without sacrificing comfort and impressions. As a result, our expenses turned out to be quite modest compared to if we had gone on vacation to Thailand or another resort. We spent about 20 000 rubles on gasoline, and expenses on housing amounted to approximately 30 000 rubles, considering that we often spent the night in a car or in a tent. We have a large car with a spacious trunk, so there was no discomfort. We took a minimum of things; the essentials fit into a couple of backpacks.

Of course, in addition to food and accommodation, You should not forget about the safety of the trip. We took out travel insurance because we were rafting on the river. Before the trip, we checked the entire car, did maintenance and diagnostics. Just in case, we bought the minimum necessary medications. We also told our loved ones that we were going on a trip and downloaded the Zenly app so they could know where we were. We also had a money cushion for emergencies. As a result, we did not need either insurance or medications, but such simple actions make us feel safe. When You have thought of everything in advance, You can enjoy the trip.

Previously, we preferred beach holidays; we lived for many months in Vietnam, Thailand, and visited many resorts in Europe. We had prejudices about traveling around Russia; we thought it was more expensive and not so interesting. But we were wrong. It turns out that we don’t know our country at all, it surprised us with its landscapes, kind people, and accessibility. If it weren't for the pandemic, we would not have decided to take such a trip. But after this trip we are sure that we will visit other places in Russia. We would like to go to Altai, visit Vladivostok, go to the south of the country. Even though we were there, traveling by car gives a completely different experience.
